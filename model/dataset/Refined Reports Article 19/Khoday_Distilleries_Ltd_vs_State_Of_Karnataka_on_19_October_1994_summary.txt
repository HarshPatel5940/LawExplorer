The case of Khoday Distilleries Ltd vs State of Karnataka was heard by the Supreme Court of India on October 19, 1994. The case involved a challenge to the constitutional validity of various rules and regulations related to the manufacture, sale, and distribution of liquor in the state of Karnataka. The main question before the court was whether the appellants/petitioners had a fundamental right to carry on trade in liquor. The court held that the right to carry on trade or business in liquor is not an absolute right and is subject to reasonable restrictions imposed by the state in the interests of the general public. The court also recognized that the state has the power to prohibit trades that are illegal, immoral, or injurious to public health and welfare. In conclusion, the Supreme Court of India upheld the constitutional validity of the rules and regulations related to the manufacture, sale, and distribution of liquor in Karnataka.
