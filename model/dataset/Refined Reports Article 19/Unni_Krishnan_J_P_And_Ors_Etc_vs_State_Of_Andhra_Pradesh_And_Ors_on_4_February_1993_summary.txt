Case Citation: Unni Krishnan, J.P. and Ors. vs State of Andhra Pradesh and Ors., Supreme Court of India, February 4, 1993, Case No. Writ Petition (Civil) No. 930 of 1990

Legal proposition: The case dealt with the right to education as a fundamental right under the Indian Constitution and addressed issues related to fees, admissions, and regulations in private educational institutions.

Case History: The case was brought before the Supreme Court of India after a Full Bench of the High Court awarded punishment to students for irregularities committed by the managements of private unaided recognized affiliated educational institutions. The students argued that they had not done anything contrary to the law and appealed the decision.

Legal Issues/Questions Presented:
1. Whether the right to education is a fundamental right under the Indian Constitution?
2. What are the limits and obligations of private recognized/affiliated educational institutions in terms of fees, admissions, and adherence to regulations?
3. Whether the prohibition of capitation fees in various state enactments is constitutional?

Applicable Legal Provisions:
- Article 21 of the Indian Constitution, which guarantees the right to life and personal liberty.
- Article 45 of the Indian Constitution, which emphasizes the importance of providing free and compulsory education to children.
- Article 14 of the Indian Constitution, which guarantees the right to equality.

Holding(s):
1. The citizens of India have a fundamental right to education, which flows from Article 21 of the Indian Constitution.
2. Every child/citizen has a right to free education until the age of 14 years. After that, the right to education is subject to the limits of economic capacity and the development of the state.
3. Private unaided recognized affiliated educational institutions are entitled to charge a fee higher than government institutions, but the fee cannot exceed a certain limit.
4. Private recognized/affiliated educational institutions are obliged to act fairly and in accordance with the conditions of grant of recognition and affiliation.
5. The prohibition of capitation fees in various state enactments is constitutional as they do not offend Article 14 of the Indian Constitution.

Legal Reasoning/Rationale:
The court held that the right to education is implicit under Article 21 of the Indian Constitution and flows from the right to life and personal liberty. The court emphasized the importance of education and the obligations of the state to provide free and compulsory education to children. The court also recognized the role of private educational institutions but set guidelines for fees, admissions, and adherence to regulations to prevent commercialization of education.

Rule of Law/Legal Principle Established or Applied:
The case established the right to education as a fundamental right and set guidelines for private educational institutions in terms of fees, admissions, and adherence to regulations. The court emphasized the importance of education and the obligations of the state to provide free and compulsory education to children.

Concurring and/or Dissenting Opinions:
There were no dissenting opinions in this case.

Implications and Significance:
The case established the right to education as a fundamental right and set guidelines for private educational institutions. It emphasized the importance of education and the obligations of the state to provide free and compulsory education to children. The case also addressed the issue of capitation fees and prohibited their charging by private educational institutions. The decision has significant implications for the education sector in India and ensures access to education for all citizens.

Comments or Analysis:
The case highlights the importance of education as a fundamental right and the role of private educational institutions in providing education. It sets guidelines for fees, admissions, and adherence to regulations to prevent commercialization of education. The decision has been instrumental in ensuring access to education for all citizens and promoting equality in the education sector.
